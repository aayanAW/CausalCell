INTRODUCTION (~5 min) - motivate from the paper's introduction plus solid background:
- S1 Title (~0.5 min): full title, my name, citation; use GraphicalAbstract.jpg as the visual.
- S2 (~1 min): HOOK - cells live as time-evolving ecosystems; modern omics gives static SNAPSHOTS, not the

- S3 (~1.5 min): Agent-based models CAN predict tissue dynamics, but the biology is buried in C++ code,
  locked away from the biologists who actually understand it. State this barrier plainly. (Use Figure1.)
- S4 (~1 min): THE QUESTION - what if a biologist could build a working model just by writing plain-English
  sentences? Set up the paper's goal.
- S5 (~1 min): THE ANSWER - the "cell behavior hypothesis grammar." Show the rule template verbatim:
  "In [cell type], [signal] increases/decreases [behavior]."  Example: "In MCF-7 cells, cisplatin increases
  apoptosis." (Use the relevant panel of Figure1_grammar.jpg.)

METHODS / RESULTS / DISCUSSION (~14 min):
- S6 (~1.5 min): HOW a sentence becomes math - each rule is a Hill (dose-response) curve; multiple rules
  combine (up-signals vs down-signals) and interpolate; rules are additive (add one without touching the
  others). Teach dose-response with a simple everyday analogy. (Figure1_grammar.jpg response-function panel.)
- S7 (~1.5 min): THE ENGINE - PhysiCell agent-based simulator; rules typed into a CSV (one row per rule);
  real omics data sets which cells exist and where (cell-type labels -> agents; spatial coordinates ->
  positions). (Figure1_grammar.jpg workflow panel.)
- S8 (~1.5 min): EXPERIMENT 1 - hypoxia. Method: oxygen-dependent rules ("oxygen decreases necrosis,"
  "oxygen decreases transformation to motile cells"). Walk Figure2_hypoxia.jpg: snapshots + oxygen field
  over time, and population-vs-time / radial-distribution plots (X = time or radius, Y = cell count/density);
  box the invasive front and the necrotic core. Result: necrotic core forms; cells turn motile and invade;
  motility is the most sensitive parameter.
- S9 (~1.5 min): EXPERIMENT 2 - fibroblasts push pancreatic-cancer cells to invade. Define CAF and EMT.
  Walk Figure3_PDAC_fibroblasts.jpg: invasiveness vs tumor:CAF ratio over time; box the 10:1 / 5:1 peak;
  note significance vs tumor-only controls.
- S10 (~2 min): EXPERIMENT 3 - emergent immune evasion. Method: add CD8+ T cells (contact killing) and
  switchable M1/M2 macrophages. Walk Figure4_tumor_immune_evasion.jpg: tumor-volume-vs-time curve dips
  >50% then REBOUNDS to baseline by day 5 as T cells exhaust and macrophages turn M2; arrow the dip-then-
  rebound. Meaning: immunosuppression emerged from simple rules, was not programmed in.
- S11 (~1.5 min): EXPERIMENT 4a - "go vs grow." Method: test whether EGF makes tumor cells DIVIDE (grow) or
  MOVE (go). Walk the in-silico panels of Figure5_go_vs_grow_validation.jpg: the grow-only hypothesis fails
  to reproduce invasion; go and go+grow reproduce it.
- S12 (~1.5 min): EXPERIMENT 4b - WET-LAB VALIDATION of the model's prediction. Walk Figure5: gefitinib
  (an EGFR inhibitor) cuts invasive protrusions in real organoids; EGF raises MCF10A cell motility. This is
  the predict-then-confirm payoff - emphasize that the computer made a testable prediction that the bench
  confirmed.
- S13 (~2 min): EXPERIMENT 5 - a VIRTUAL CLINICAL TRIAL in pancreatic cancer. Method: build "virtual
  patients" from patient scRNA-seq, then simulate each drug's mechanism. Walk Figure6_virtual_clinical_
  trial.jpg: large patient-to-patient variation in response; responders had higher macrophage abundance
  (Pearson correlation) -> NEW hypothesis: macrophages must clear tumor cells first so T cells can then kill.
- S14 (~1.5 min): GENERALIZATION beyond cancer - the same grammar builds mouse-brain cortical layers, fit to
  the Allen Brain Atlas. Walk Figure7_brain_cortex.jpg: layer-thickness model-vs-data for two cortical
  regions (somatosensory and auditory). Message: this is a general tissue tool, not a cancer-only trick.

CONCLUSION (~1 min):
- S15 (~0.5 min): THREE TAKEAWAYS - plain English becomes real models; it integrates multi-omics data;
